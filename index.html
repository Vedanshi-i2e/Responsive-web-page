What to do if...

Select the topic below to see what you should do in each scenario

If you identify a potential external opportunity during a meeting, it's crucial to document the details immediately. Note the nature of the opportunity, the stakeholders involved, and any relevant timelines. After the meeting, reach out to your supervisor or the appropriate department within WRD to discuss the next steps and how to formally present the opportunity.
To understand WRD's partnering areas of interest, consult the company's partnership strategy documents or reach out to the Business Development team. They can provide insights into current initiatives, preferred partners, and the strategic goals that guide partnerships. This knowledge will help align potential collaborations with WRD's objectives.
When approached by a non-employee regarding an external opportunity, ensure that you gather comprehensive information about the proposal. Encourage them to submit a formal inquiry or proposal through the appropriate channels. Report this interaction to your manager to evaluate the potential alignment with WRD's interests and compliance with company policies.
